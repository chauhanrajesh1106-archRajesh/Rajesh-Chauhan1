Introduction Of Swami Vivekanandan

Swami Vivekananda was an influential Indian Hindu monk, philosopher, and spiritual leader who introduced Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the West in the late 19th century. Born Narendranath Datta in 1863, he was a chief disciple of the mystic Ramakrishna and is renowned for his 1893 speech at the World's Parliament of Religions in Chicago. Vivekananda was also a key figure in the revival of Hinduism in India and founded the Ramakrishna Math and the Ramakrishna mission

Character Sketch Of Swami Vivekanand

Swami Vivekananda was an inspiring, multifaceted personality: a great spiritual leader, philosopher, social reformer, and patriot. He was known for his strong intellect, eloquent oratory skills, and unwavering patriotism and respect for Indian culture. He was a devout follower of Sri Ramakrishna, dedicated to social service through the Ramakrishna Mission and spreading the wisdom of Vedanta globally.

Spiritual leader

He was a chief disciple of Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, and through his lectures and writings, he spread the principles of Vedanta and Yoga throughout the world.
